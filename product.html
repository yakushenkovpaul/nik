      Overview of The Deck Patong in Patong, Phuket

      The Deck Patong is a condo and apartment project located in Patong, Phuket and was completed in Aug 2015. It has 270 units across 7 floors and was developed by Sansiri, who is also behind FLO by Sansiri , The Muve Srinakarin and The Base Sukhumvit 71.

      About The Deck Patong

      The Deck Patong, a luxurious condominium in a potential location of the Patong area. The building architecture is meticulously designed in a modern style but remains elegant with a charming touch of Phuket. The project completes a full range of facilities such as a fully-equipped gym, a swimming pool, and many more. Let every step be convenient with surrounding amenities. Just a few minutes to Jungceylon Department Store, Central Patong, and Patong Beach, a world-famous tourist destination. Fascinated by the nightlife colors of Bangla Road, a home for famous pubs, bars, and nightclubs such as Illusion. All of these are waiting for you at The Deck Patong.

      Project Information
      The Deck Patong is located at 81 Rat U Thit 200 Pee Road, Patong, Kathu, Phuket. It is a project development from Sansiri, which was completed in 2015. The project is a low-rise condominium of 2 buildings, 7 floors with 270 residential units.

      Direction
      To get to the project by car, start from the bypass road, heading southwest on Route 4042 for 3.9 kilometers. Keep right to continue on Route 4024 for 2.5 kilometers. Then, turn right at the Darasamuth Intersection to enter Route 4020. Continue for 5.5 kilometers and turn left onto Route 4029 for 3.4 kilometers. Then, turn right and stay on Route 4029 for 1.1 kilometers and turn left onto Rat-U-Thit 200 Pee Road. Continue for 260 meters; you will find the project on the left.

      Foreigners can own a condominium Freehold in their own name. Condominium ownership is best explained by looking at an example of a project that has 100 units for sale.

      In this example, 49 of the total units in this building (or 49% of the total salable area) can be owned outright by non-thai nationals in their name.  The ownership titles of these 49 units is referred to as Freehold or Foreign Quota. 

      This leaves 51 units remaining in the building (or 51% of the total salable). The freehold rights to these units are reserved for Thai nationals in what is commonly referred to as the Thai quota.
